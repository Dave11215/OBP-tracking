How it's driven

  • Recovery and hard/easy balance set the order, not the day of week. The calendar is one sound arrangement, not a fixed template — slide sessions by how recovered you are.
  • Weekends are your outdoor days. Long Run, Track intervals, and long Zone 2 rides on Sat/Sun; rest and strength on weekdays. Droppable if recovery needs a weekend rest.
  • Polarized: most sessions easy, usually two genuinely hard per week, a third only on a well-recovered stretch.
  • Run + bike mix: running carries the higher-intensity work; the bike carries joint-sparing Zone 2 volume and is the impact-free hard option.
  • Autoregulate by HRV trend: a hard session goes only when the trend is flat/up and sleep was decent; if it dips, that slot becomes easy or rest.

Sequencing rules

  • Never two hard days back-to-back. The two hard sessions sit ~3 days apart; the weekend pairs a hard Saturday with an easy long ride Sunday.
  • Long Run on fresh-ish legs: not the day after leg strength (C) or after a hard run.
  • Track / speed ~weekly is your economy driver; long runs progress ~a mile a week; Bike VO2 takes one slot to spare impact.
  • Usually two hard, occasionally three — a third only on a genuinely well-recovered stretch, never on feel alone.

Running sessions

Easy Run + strides — Zone 2

30–40 min conversational (HR ~125–140, cap 145), then 4–6 x 10–15s strides. Keep it genuinely easy.

Quality Run — midweek hard (gated)

5K tempo / threshold. Warm up, finish strong but not to failure, cadence high. Swap to Bike VO2 if legs/HRV need it.

Track intervals — speed (gated)

WU 12–15 min + drills, then 5–6 x 400–800 m at ~5K effort (HR to upper Z4, cap ~172) with generous recovery, CD. Never a time trial.

Long Run — weekend endurance

Easy-to-moderate, progressing ~a mile a week (~4 → 5 → 6 → 6–7). Outdoors on the weekend.

Bike sessions

Zone 2 Bike — easy base, joint-sparing

Conversational Zone 2, 40–80 min. Long outdoor ride on the weekend, or a short spin midweek.

Bike VO2 — impact-free hard

WU 10–12 min, intervals at ~90–95% max HR (3–4 min on / 3–4 easy), CD. Redline is fine, no impact. Never to failure.

Prehab routines

Knees + ankle/foot ride with your runs (do them as a quick pre-run activation) and strength days; shoulders/elbows is a daily near-freebie. A few minutes each.
Shoulders / Elbows — daily, ~3–5 min
  • Reverse Tyler Twist · for the right common-flexor tendon, 2 x 12-15
  • Band external + internal rotation · 2 x 12/side
  • Scapular / face pulls · 2 x 12-15
  • Hammer eccentric pronation + supination · slow, 2 x 10/side

Protects the right elbow (partial common-flexor tear + golfer's elbow) and shoulder. Keep sub-painful.

Knees — run + strength days (pre-run activation), ~3–5 min
  • Step-ups · controlled, 2 x 10/leg
  • Eccentric step-downs · slow single-leg lower, 2 x 8/leg
  • Star taps · single-leg reach in 3 directions, 2 rounds/leg
  • Wall sit · 2 x 30-45s
  • Terminal knee extension (band) · 2 x 15

Maintenance for the knee (the strength-before-running gate that closed the recurrence cycle). Cadence toward 165+ is the #1 running-mechanics fix alongside this.

Ankle / Foot — run + strength days, ~5 min (trail-driven)
  • Single-leg balance · 30s/leg; eyes open → closed → on a cushion
  • Band eversion (peroneals) · slow, 2 x 15/leg — resists the ankle-roll
  • Eccentric calf raise · 3s lower, 2-3 x 10/leg (straight + bent knee)
  • Single-leg RDL reach · 2 x 8/leg
  • Knee-to-wall dorsiflexion · 2 x 10/leg
  • Short-foot / toe splay · 5 x 5s hold

Ankle stability for trail. Add hop-and-stick (2 x 5/leg) from week 3. The left ankle is a running abort signal.

Daily anchors: morning-light walk · protein-forward eating · hydration + salt. Standing rules: no overhead pressing · no sit-ups · elbow-friendly grips · power moves first, driven fast · never train to failure (RIR 1–2) · keep the easy run genuinely easy · abort a run on any calf / Achilles / hamstring / knee / left-ankle signal · don't stack alcohol + a fatty late meal on a hard-training evening.
