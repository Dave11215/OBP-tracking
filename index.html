The 30-day plan

Colored pill = what you actually did. Gray line under it = what was prescribed. Tap any day to log it.
✓ green check = you made the right call for the day. Earned by: training as prescribed, adapting sensibly, resting when rest was right, or resting because you are sick or hurt (that is the highest form of honoring the day, and it can never break the streak).
The streak breaks on only two things: (1) a day with no decision at all — you drifted, you did not choose; (2) training through a red gate — overriding a clear no-go. Yes, that means a workout can break the streak. The streak measures judgment, not volume.
Honesty clause: the log must be what actually happened. A day quietly reclassified after the fact is a broken streak. The data is the asset.

How it's driven

  • Recovery and hard/easy balance set the order, not the day of week. The calendar is one sound arrangement, not a fixed template — slide sessions by how recovered you are.
  • Adapting is not failing. Swapping a session, or turning a hard day into rest when life or recovery demands it, is the system working. Log what you actually did and keep the check.
  • Weekends are your outdoor days. Long Run, Track intervals, and long Zone 2 rides on Sat/Sun; rest and strength on weekdays.
  • Polarized: most sessions easy, usually two genuinely hard per week, a third only on a well-recovered stretch.
  • Run + bike mix: running carries the higher-intensity work; the bike carries joint-sparing Zone 2 volume and is the impact-free hard option.
  • Autoregulate by HRV trend: a hard session goes only when the trend is flat/up and sleep was decent; if it dips, that slot becomes easy or rest.

Sequencing rules

  • Never two hard days back-to-back. The two hard sessions sit ~3 days apart; the weekend pairs a hard Saturday with an easy long ride Sunday.
  • Long Run on fresh-ish legs: not the day after leg strength (C) or after a hard run.
  • Track / speed ~weekly is your economy driver; long runs progress ~a mile a week; Bike VO2 takes one slot to spare impact.
  • Stack, do not smear. Two sessions on one day (strength AM + easy Z2 PM) so the rest days can be genuinely empty. Concentrate the stress; protect the recovery. Keep the PM session truly easy, 6+ hours apart, priority session first.
  • Strength 2x/week is a FLOOR. It is the muscle-preservation anchor of the recomp. If the schedule cannot fit it, fix the schedule, not the floor.
  • Never more than ~5 consecutive training days without a rest day. Recovery is half of what produced the HRV gain; a long unbroken block quietly drops that half.
  • Usually two hard, occasionally three — a third only on a genuinely well-recovered stretch, never on feel alone.

Running sessions

Easy Run + strides — Zone 2

30–40 min conversational (HR ~125–140, cap 145), then 4–6 x 10–15s strides. Keep it genuinely easy.

Quality Run — midweek hard (gated)

5K tempo / threshold. Warm up, finish strong but not to failure, cadence high. Swap to Bike VO2 if legs/HRV need it.

Track intervals — speed (gated)

WU 12–15 min + drills, then 5–6 x 400–800 m at ~5K effort (HR to upper Z4, cap ~172) with generous recovery, CD. Never a time trial.

Long Run — weekend endurance

Easy-to-moderate, progressing ~a mile a week (~4 → 5 → 6 → 6–7). Outdoors on the weekend.

Bike sessions

Zone 2 Bike — easy base, joint-sparing

Conversational Zone 2, HR 120–140 (soft cap 145), 45–90 min. Long outdoor ride on the weekend; carbs + salted fluid past ~75 min.

Bike VO2 — impact-free hard

WU 10–12 min, intervals at ~90–95% max HR (3–4 min on / 3–4 easy), CD. Redline is fine, no impact. Never to failure. This is where the over-threshold work lives.
